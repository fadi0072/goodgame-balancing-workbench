# Track B — Drive & Gap-Handle a Pre-Built Balancing Workbench

Welcome, and thanks for your time. This is a take-home structured around three short phases.

## Total time cap

**~3.5 hours.** Phase 1 ~30 min, Phase 2 ~2-2.5 h, Phase 3 ~30-60 min.

---

## Phase 1 — Read & critique a real skill (~30 min)

Attached: `phase-1/SKILL_TO_CRITIQUE.md` — a sanitised version of one of our actual specialist skills.

**Deliverable:** ~1-page write-up answering four questions:

1. In your own words, what does this skill do?
2. What's well-designed about it?
3. What would you change or add?
4. What assumptions does it make about the project around it?

---

## Phase 2 — Drive & gap-handle (~2-2.5 h)

You'll receive a fully pre-built mini-workbench in the `workbench/` directory:

- A working `CLAUDE.md` orienting you to the project
- Three working stub skills: `apply-changes-lite`, `qa-check-lite`, `audit-change-lite`
- A `context/known-overrides.md` documenting one server-side override
- A `context/file-mappings.md` documenting the data file's schema
- The synthetic `data/Src_Hero_Data.txt`
- Four change requests in `change_requests.md` (producer voice)

**Your task:** apply all four changes through the workbench, each as its own atomic commit with a decision-log entry. If you hit a wall, identify what's missing in the workbench, propose how you'd extend it, and execute as best you can with what's available.

We're more interested in **how you handle a gap** than whether you bridge it perfectly.

**Deliverables:**

1. **Forked GitHub repo** with all four changes attempted, atomic commits, per-change decision-log entries.
2. **Screen recording, 10-15 minutes, voice-over, unedited** — you driving Claude through **Change #4 specifically** (the hardest one). Tool of your choice.
3. **Half-page written gap-diagnosis** — what's missing from this workbench, how you'd fix it.

---

## Phase 3 — Written reflection (~30-60 min, ~1 page)

Answer these four prompts in writing:

1. Walk us through Change #4. What did you identify as missing in the workbench, and how did you decide what to do?
2. Show us a project (work or personal) where you've extended an AI tool beyond its default behaviour. What did you build, and why?
3. What's your AI-tool stack today, and what are you actively experimenting with that you haven't shipped?
4. If you took this role, what would your first 30 days look like?

---

## Submission

Send your GitHub repo link, the video link, the Phase 1 write-up, the Phase 2 gap-diagnosis, and the Phase 3 reflection to the contact you've been working with. We'll review and follow up within ~5 business days.

Good luck.
